What is Virtual Reality?

VR is an abbreviation of virtual reality and has the purpose of simulating a realistic experience through computer technology. To experience virtual reality, you use a headset. If you are playing a game, you use game controllers as well. Features as sound and other sensations can be present too, to stimulate the users physical presence in an virtual environment.

Virtual Reality in Hospitals

According to a study done at the Children's Hospital Los Angeles, Virtual Reality is effective in reducing pain during certain medical procedures, and it helps children to manage pain without the use of drugs. It significantly reduces acute procedural pain and anxiety, compared to using the standard of care.

Distraction & Entertainment

We make hospital visits more fun and enjoyable.

Simple medical procedures, such as getting blood drawn or vaccination, can be very stressful and anxiety-provoking for a child. In these situations, playing a simple game in virtual reality can distract the child, help them relax and even reduce the pain. This is not just beneficial for the child, but also the parents, doctors and nurses, who are often very busy.

Hospital stays does not have to be boring.

Virtual reality is a great way to escape from the real world and take your mind off of things. VR games functions as entertainment for those unfortunate kids who are hospitalized for a longer period of time. There are games for children of all ages and the VR games are all designed to be relaxing, positive and happy. They can go to the zoo, play minigolf, explore space or even go for a walk in the park with their virtual puppy, which they can feed, pet and teach tricks.
